Leonardo Davinchi,Mona Lisa

Painted between 1503 and 1517, Da Vinci’s alluring portrait has been dogged by two questions since the day it was made: Who’s the subject and why is she smiling? A number of theories for the former have been proffered over the years: That she’s the wife of the Florentine merchant Francesco di Bartolomeo del Giocondo (ergo, the work’s alternative title, La Gioconda); that she's Leonardo’s mother, Caterina, conjured from Leonardo's boyhood memories of her; and finally, that it's a self-portrait in drag. As for that famous smile, its enigmatic quality has driven people crazy for centuries. Whatever the reason, Mona Lisa’s look of preternatural calm comports with the idealized landscape behind her, which dissolves into the distance through Leonardo’s use of atmospheric perspective.






Vincent Van Goh,The Starry Night

Created in June 1889. Housed in the Museum of Modern Art, New York, the post-impressionist masterpiece depicts a swirling night sky, vibrant stars, and a tranquil village. Van Gogh's expressive brushstrokes convey both the beauty of the night and his inner emotional struggles. The painting is celebrated for its unique style and profound symbolism.

Michaelangelo,The Creation Of Adam

"The Creation of Adam" is a fresco painted by Michelangelo on the Sistine Chapel ceiling around 1512. The iconic artwork depicts the biblical scene of God reaching out to Adam, their hands nearly touching. The composition is celebrated for its emotional depth, conveying the moment of divine impartation of life. The painting is a masterpiece of Renaissance art, showcasing Michelangelo's exceptional skill and creativity.
